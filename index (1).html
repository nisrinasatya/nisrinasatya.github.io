CEO Request Tracking System

What I worked on

The CEO's office received requests from across the organization with no consistent system for logging, prioritizing, or following up. I designed a centralized intake and tracking system from scratch — adopted organization-wide as the standard process.

How I think & execute

I started by auditing what was breaking: requests arrived across multiple channels (Teams, email, verbal), got lost, or were duplicated. Organization-wide adoption confirmed the design was right.

Results

Scenario

A fictional e-learning company (LearnPath Global) needed Indonesian localization for a 3-module onboarding course. I simulated the full LPM cycle — from brief intake to delivery sign-off.

What I did

  • Created a project brief template capturing scope, word count, deadline, tone, and glossary requirements
  • Built a Gantt-style project timeline in Google Sheets with translation, review, QA, and delivery stages
  • Wrote a vendor evaluation scorecard across 5 criteria: language quality, CAT tool proficiency, domain knowledge, responsiveness, price
  • Drafted a QA checklist covering terminology, style compliance, formatting, and cultural appropriateness

How I think & execute

The most common failure in localization projects is scope creep and last-minute changes. I designed the brief intake process specifically to capture everything upfront — tone, audience, glossary, file format expectations — so there were no surprises at delivery.
